History

On January 16, 1966, Chicago was granted an NBA franchise to be called the Bulls. The Chicago Bulls became the third NBA franchise in the city. The Bulls' founder, Dick Klein, was the Bulls' only owner to ever play professional basketball (for the Chicago American Gears). He served as the Bulls' president and general manager in their initial years. After the 1966 NBA Expansion Draft, the newly founded Chicago Bulls were allowed to acquire players from the previously established teams in the league for the upcoming 1966–67 season. The team started during the 1966–67 NBA season, and posted a 33–48 record, the best by an expansion team in NBA history.

Trophies

There are 6 NBA trophies earned by the Bulls. Being the first team to win the season three consecutive times on two occasions. In the 1991 season (vs Lakers winning 4-1), 1992 (vs Trail Blazers winning 4-2), 1993 (vs Suns winning 4-2), 1996 (vs SuperSonics winning 4-2), 1997 (vs Jazz winning 4-2) and finally 1998 (vs Jazz winning 4-2). Also individual awards like Mvp of the season achieved by Michael Jordan - 1988, 1991, 1992, 1996, 1998 and Derrick Rose - 2011. Rookie of the Year Michael Jordan - 1985, Elton Brand - 2000 (Co-Rookie of the Year) and Derrick Rose - 2009. All Star Game MVP Michael Jordan - 1988, 1996, 1998 and Scottie Pippen - 1994.

Best Team

The 95-96 season team set the record for the most wins in a season, winning 72 games and only 10 losses.This record lasted 20 years, until the Golden State Warriors, in the 2015-16 season, surpassed it with 73 victories, although they did not win the title .The squad was made up of Randy Brown, Jud Buechler, Jason Caffey, James Edwards, Jack Haley, Ron Harper, Michael Jordan, Steve Kerr, Toni Kukoc, Luc Longley, Scottie Pippen, Dennis Rodman, John Salley, Dickey Simpkins and Bill Wennington.

Michael Jordan

Most important player of the club crowning himself 6 times champion of the NBA, achieving 6 times the MVP award for the finals and 5 times MVP for the season. In addition to his 2 Olympic gold medals. Michael Jeffrey Jordan (New York; February 17, 1963) is a former American basketball player. He currently owns the NBA team the Charlotte Hornets. He retired permanently in 2003 at the Washington Wizards, having previously done on two previous occasions, in 1993 and 1999, after playing 13 seasons with the Chicago Bulls.
